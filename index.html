OUR STORY

Step 1

At the beginning, we were inspired by Bruno Simon's work on the "Car Visualizer" website. We thought, "Why not create something equally impressive?" So, we came up with the idea of building an island with three houses, each representing us.

step 2

After that, we began planning the project's structure and distributing tasks among each of us, outlining the necessary steps to kickstart the project.

step 3

We used Blender software to integrate the models and design the rooms. We also added objects to the screens where we would display the content, and we determined the camera locations. Additionally, we utilized models from Sketchfab, arranging them within the rooms and completing the island's setup.

step 4

After that, we started working on the backend, creating admin pages to control each room, along with attached chats and Google login/logout functionality. We added curl for additional modification and deletion, and dealt with Firebase databases. Our first experience with relational NoSQL databases taught us valuable lessons, but we faced challenges with downloading numerous packages and managing multiple chats.

step 5

Next, we began the process of assembling and integrating models into the website using Three.js and the React framework, both of which were new to us. While it was an exciting experience, we faced numerous challenges along the way. These included memory leaks due to the size of the models, difficulties in loading certain models or loading them incorrectly, texture issues, lighting and object placement problems, and overall slow loading times in the browser.

step 6

To fix the memory leak, we rebuilt the models and started over from scratch, making sure they fit well with the code. We also added a loading page to speed up loading times.
